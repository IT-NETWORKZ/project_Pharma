Advanced Control Strategies for Nitrosamine Impurities

Nitrosamine Impurities Overview

We conduct focused, safety-driven R&D on N-nitrosamines and related nitroso impurities to protect patient safety, ensure regulatory compliance and support our customers (API and formulation manufacturers) in managing these compounds across the supply chain.

Our work covers the full lifecycle: Risk Assessment ‚Üí Analytical Method Development ‚Üí Control & Mitigation Strategies ‚Üí Stability & Batch Monitoring ‚Üí Regulatory Documentation.

Examples of compounds we study include: NDMA, NDEA, NEIPA, NDIPA, NDBA, NMBA, NPYR, NPIP, NMOR, NDPA, NDPhA, NMPhA, NEMA, NBnMA, NDBnA, NDELA, NDIBA, NEPhA, DNPIPZ, N-nitrosomethylpropylamine, N-nitrosomethylisopropylamine, N-nitrosomethyl-tert-butylamine, NMU, MNNG, MNPIPZ, NDCYCA, N-nitrosovarenicline, N-nitroso vortioxetine and vortioxetine sulfoxide.

Our R&D Focus Areas

  • Systematic risk screening of APIs, intermediates, excipients, solvents, reagents and manufacturing steps that could lead to nitrosamine formation or carry-over.
  • Assessment covers potential sources such as nitrosating agents, amine precursors, solvent/reagent impurities and processing/storage conditions.
  • We perform root-cause investigations for any detected nitrosamine to identify origin and corrective actions.
Regulatory expectation: marketing authorization holders must assess nitrosamine risk in products and manufacturing processes.

  • Development and validation of robust, stability-indicating analytical methods for trace-level quantitation (GC/MS, LC-MS/MS and other orthogonal techniques), following regulatory validation principles.
  • Emphasis on sensitivity (low ng/g or ng/mL levels where required), method specificity, accuracy, precision and system suitability reporting.
  • Routine method suitability checks and participation in external proficiency tests where available.
Regulators expect validated methods capable of reliably detecting nitrosamines at or below acceptable intake levels.

  • Use of published AIs and, where appropriate, compound-specific toxicological data, read-across or SAR approaches to derive acceptable intake limits.
  • When compound-specific AIs are absent, we follow current international approaches (FDA/EMA/ICH/EDQM recommendations) to propose scientifically justified limits.
See FDA and EMA recommended acceptable intake approaches and lists of established AIs.

  • Process redesign and raw-material controls to eliminate or minimize formation pathways.
  • Supplier qualification and detailed incoming material testing for amines, nitrite and related precursors.
  • Specification updates, tightened storage/handling SOPs and change control to prevent new risks.
  • Use of preventive controls and CAPA following detection.
Regulatory guidance expects risk-based controls and supplier management to prevent nitrosamine presence.

  • Longitudinal monitoring of retained samples to evaluate nitrosamine formation over shelf life and under stress/storage conditions.
  • Batch release includes nitrosamine screening where risk assessment indicates potential presence.
  • Ongoing surveillance and trending to detect shifts in impurity profiles.

  • Preparation of risk assessment reports, method validation packages and corrective action documents for regulatory submissions and inspections.
  • Support for dossier updates, variations and responses to regulatory queries.
  • We align documentation to ICH M7 principles and regional positions (FDA, EMA, EDQM).

  • Strict laboratory safety, chemical hygiene and waste-management practices for working with nitroso compounds and suspected precursors.
  • Personnel receive targeted training on hazard awareness, sample handling and emergency procedures.
